Business Structure Selection

LLC (Recommended)

Best for 90% of contractors

Advantages:

  • Limited liability protection
  • Tax flexibility (pass-through or corporate)
  • Simple formation and maintenance
  • No double taxation
  • Credibility with customers and suppliers

Typical Costs:

$50-$500 state filing fee + registered agent (~$100/year)

S-Corporation Election

When profits exceed $75K

Advantages:

  • Reduces self-employment taxes
  • Reasonable salary + distributions
  • Potential tax savings of $3,000-$10,000+
  • Can elect with existing LLC

Requirements:

Must pay yourself reasonable salary, additional payroll taxes and accounting complexity

Sole Proprietorship

High Risk

Why to Avoid:

  • No liability protection
  • Personal assets at risk
  • Difficult to get business credit
  • Less professional appearance
  • Higher insurance costs

Only Consider If:

Testing the market with very low-risk services only

State Licensing Requirements

Licensing requirements vary dramatically by state and trade. Here's what you need to know:

üü¢ Texas (Business-Friendly)

$2,500 average licensing cost
  • No state-level general contractor license
  • Local jurisdiction requirements only
  • Trade-specific licenses required
  • Business registration: $300

üü° Florida (Moderate)

$4,200 average licensing cost
  • State licensing required
  • Financial responsibility requirements
  • 4-hour business law exam
  • Trade exam required

üî¥ California (Strict)

$11,350 average licensing cost
  • $25,000 contractor license bond required
  • 4 years journey-level experience
  • Workers' compensation mandatory
  • Extensive examination requirements

Federal Requirements

EIN (Employer Identification Number)

Cost: Free from IRS (beware of scam sites charging fees)

Required for: LLCs, hiring employees, business bank accounts

How to get: Apply online at IRS.gov (takes 15 minutes)

SAM Registration (Government Contracting)

Cost: Free (required for federal contracts)

Benefits: Access to 23% of federal contracts set aside for small businesses

Timeline: 10-15 business days for approval

OSHA Compliance

Companies with 11+ employees: Must maintain OSHA 300 logs

All contractors: Report fatalities within 8 hours

Required programs: Fall protection, hazard communication, PPE

EPA RRP Rule

Applies to: Work on pre-1978 housing

Certification cost: $300 initial + $200 renewal every 5 years

Violations: Up to $37,500 per violation

Insurance Requirements

General Liability

$1M per occurrence minimum
$1,200-$4,000/year
  • Protects against property damage claims
  • Covers bodily injury liability
  • Required by most general contractors
  • Often bundled with commercial auto

Workers' Compensation

Required in most states
$2,000-$8,000/year
  • Mandatory when you hire employees
  • Covers medical costs and lost wages
  • Rates vary by trade classification
  • California requires for specific trades

Commercial Auto

$1M liability minimum
$1,500-$3,500/year
  • Covers business use of vehicles
  • Tools and equipment coverage
  • Higher limits than personal auto
  • Required for commercial plates

Professional Liability

$1M recommended
$800-$2,500/year
  • Covers design errors (design-build)
  • Professional advice liability
  • Increasingly required for larger projects
  • Cyber liability add-on available
